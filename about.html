About.

Ryn Escarra Cypher is a strategic research consultant working with non-profit organizations, teams, & mission-driven solopreneurs nationwide. Since forming her own practice in 2018, she has signed on to projects that share a commitment to social good and demonstrably deliver on that promise. Prior to consulting, she gained hands-on experience working in web development, informatics, and project management, exemplifying adaptability and growth potential in highly conceptual and analytical roles.


Earlier in her career, Ryn served as lab manager for the Emotion and Cognition Laboratory where she oversaw two grants from the National Institute of Health (NIH, R01) and National Science Foundation (NSF, SES), and successfully co-wrote a grant proposal for the National Institute of Health/Aging’s call for exploratory research on the mitigating effects of affect-based interventions on biomarkers of Alzheimer’s Disease progression. She has published interdisciplinary projects across various outlets, including Journal of Child and Family Studies and Journal of Experimental Psychology.


Collaborative, trustworthy and a generous team-player, Ryn maintains a “can do” attitude and focuses on achieving exceptionally high quality and detailed results for her clients. She has a strong ability to engage, evaluate and articulate motivations and organizational dynamics across sectors.


Ryn graduated summa cum laude with a bachelor’s degree in industrial-organizational psychology and completed graduate studies in human-computer interaction, information sciences, and bioinformatics from DePaul University, Northwestern University, and The University of Chicago. A current member of the Chicago Council on Global Affairs Young Professional Network and regular volunteer for local charities and events, she is actively engaged with her community, at home and abroad. Ryn lives in Chicago with her partner, two dogs, and a cat. She enjoys running, yoga, and cooking.
